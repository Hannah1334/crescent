Appearance
Arcane cultures across the multiverse depict their Night Goddess in various appearances through numerous platforms, from religious texts to poems to pieces of physical visual art. Differences between them set aside, all of them seem to agree on three primary aspects: firstly, Crescent of the Full Moon is indeed a Goddess and is always depicted as female. Secondly, her eyes are always as pale silver-white as the moon itself, and finally, her long silky locks of hair are a deep obsidian and are covered in constant projections of the constellations she currently resides beneath.
In actuality the reason imagery of her varies so vastly is because the Mother of Night can change her fabricated mortal coil’s traits at will, shifting things such as height, weight, complexion, limbs, and such at will. Her favorite appearance she’s partial to is a six foot tall woman with grey or peach skin, balancing a tiny physique with plenty of feminine curvature.

Personality
Observant and curious, especially when it comes to the worlds of humankind. Crescent is firm in her own beliefs and morals but is accurately described as being understanding and open minded, possibly even to a fault. Nonetheless she still has the capacity for wrath and cruelty befitting a Goddess of dark magics, thankfully making an enemy out of her is no easy task.

In their planes of origin Magic was not broken down to so many categories and varying trees. They fell under two specific classifications: Light and Dark. When crafting and planting the Magic Trees themselves it became apparent that both entities could cast all kinds of arcane spells under every affiliation under their more generalized specializations.
